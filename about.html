About the Author

Bradley Hobbs

Bradley Hobbs writes from lived experience, not theory. His work centers on the unseen realities of spiritual harm, the weight of gossip, and the long road toward healing and restoration.

His voice emerges from seasons where silence was learned, trust was broken, and identity was challenged in environments that were meant to be safe. Those experiences did not end his story. They shaped his calling.

Through Bruised Reeds: How Gossip Became Violence in the Church, Bradley speaks to those who have been misunderstood, misrepresented, or quietly wounded in spiritual spaces. His writing does not rush healing or minimize pain. It names what many have felt but could not articulate, and it points toward restoration that is grounded in truth, gentleness, and the presence of Jesus.

Bradley’s perspective is rooted in the belief that God is one and that His name is Jesus. His work reflects a commitment to clarity, compassion, and spiritual integrity. He writes to restore voices, to challenge harmful patterns within the church, and to create space where bruised reeds can heal, stand, and thrive.

Beyond writing, his heart is to see individuals walk in freedom, reclaim their identity, and live with purpose that is not shaped by gossip or accusation but by the truth of who they are in Christ.

This work is not just a message. It is an invitation. Healing is possible. Restoration is real. Your voice still matters.
